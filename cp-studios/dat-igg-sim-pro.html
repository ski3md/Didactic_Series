🧫 Eluate Mini‑Sim — “last wash” must be negative
Help & Reasoning

Why eluate? If IgG coats RBCs (and the patient was transfused recently or is a newborn), antibody may be detectable only **on the cells**. Elution releases bound IgG to test its specificity. The **last wash** must be **negative** to prove no residual plasma antibody contaminates the eluate.

Pre‑check

Eluate is most useful when **IgG‑DAT is positive** and there’s **recent transfusion** or **HDFN** concern.

Workflow

  1. Wash DAT‑positive RBCs ≥3× (EDTA). 0
  2. Test last wash supernatant → must be negative. Not tested
  3. If last wash is negative, perform acid elution.
  4. Test the eluate against a panel → interpret. Pending
A negative eluate with IgG‑DAT+ may suggest drug‑mediated NIPA or a low‑incidence antigen; correlate clinically.
