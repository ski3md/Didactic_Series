Help & Reasoning

21‑OH Deficiency (Pathway)

  • Block: 17‑OHP → 11‑deoxycortisol and progesterone → 11‑deoxycorticosterone.
  • Result: ↓cortisol (±↓aldosterone) → ↑ACTH, adrenal hyperplasia, ↑androgens, ↑↑17‑OHP. (screening marker)

Evidence: Merck Manual (2024), UpToDate (2025), NEJM review (2020). [1](https://journals.viamedica.pl/journal_of_transfusion_medicine_and_hemostasis/article/download/106171/84153)[2](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/374410326_Drug-Induced_Immune_Hemolytic_Anemia_Detection_of_New_Signals_and_Risk_Assessment_in_a_Nationwide_Cohort_Study/fulltext/651c320d1e2386049df3db22/Drug-Induced-Immune-Hemolytic-Anemia-Detection-of-New-Signals-and-Risk-Assessment-in-a-Nationwide-Cohort-Study.pdf)[3](https://www.uptodate.com/contents/drug-induced-hemolytic-anemia)

Clinical Presentations

  • Salt‑wasting: hyponatremia, hyperkalemia, dehydration, shock risk.
  • Simple virilizing: androgen excess without salt wasting.
  • Nonclassic: later onset mild hyperandrogenism (e.g., hirsutism, irregular menses).

Evidence: UpToDate (2025), NEJM review (2020). Compact Pathway Map

Normal: 17‑OHP → (21‑OH) → 11‑deoxycortisol → (11β‑OH) → Cortisol; Progesterone → (21‑OH) → DOC → Aldosterone.

21‑OH Deficiency: Blocked steps → ↑17‑OHP (screening marker), ↓cortisol/±↓aldosterone, shunting to androgens, ↑ACTH and adrenal hyperplasia.
